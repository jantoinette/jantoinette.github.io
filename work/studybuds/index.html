Challenge

The primary challenge was to reduce the abandonment rate during the sign-up process. Users were struggling with an overly complicated sign-up flow, unclear messaging, and a lack of visual appeal, which led to frustration and drop-offs. This led to missed opportunities for new users and poor conversion rates.

Original landing screen and input form with notes from heuristic evaluation

Problem discovery

Through heuristic evaluation, we pinpointed the problem areas of the original user journey, and discovered the following key issues:

Lack of User Control and Freedom

The design lacked important features like a “Back” button, preventing users from easily correcting mistakes or adjusting their choices, leading to frustration and abandonment.

Poor Visibility of System Status

There was no indication of progress during the sign-up process. Users were left in the dark about how many steps were remaining, creating a sense of uncertainty and disengagement.

Error Prevention

Users were not receiving confirmation messages after submitting input, which led to uncertainty about whether their actions had been successful. Additionally, input fields were not optimized, causing frustration during form completion.

System Not Matching with Real World

The system asked for payment details before users were able to fully explore or book a tutor, which created a mismatch between user expectations and the flow of the process. Users felt pressured and confused, as this was an unusual and unanticipated step at that point.

Lack of Help and Documentation

The information on the sign-up page was dense and overwhelming. There were no clear tooltips or guides, making it difficult for users to understand how to proceed. The design felt crowded, which added to user frustration.

Hypothesis and Ideation

With these insights, we hypothesized that streamlining the sign-up flow and personalizing search results for students seeking tutors online would make the process more efficient and enjoyable.

We then ideated potential solutions that addressed the discovered problems based on our hypothesis, including:

Eliminating Unnecessary Fields

We removed fields from the original form that were not essential for the immediate sign-up process and could be collected later in order to simplify the flow. This helped reduce cognitive overload and kept the process focused and clear.

Sectioning the Form into Digestible Steps

By using a step-by-step flow, users could focus on completing one field at a time without being distracted by others. This helped avoid overwhelming users, and create a more interactive experience with clearer progress tracking.

Conditional Logic for Flexibility

To give users more control over their sign-up experience, we implemented conditional logic that allowed users to skip account creation during the initial sign-up process. By offering users the freedom to complete the account creation at their own pace, we aimed to reduce abandonment rates and increase user satisfaction.

Redesigning the visual identity

The platform needed a new brand that would resonate with high school students, creating a more welcoming and engaging experience through modern typography, and a comfortable color palette.

Sketches and Wireframes

Prototyping

After initial sketches of how the UX flow of the sign up process can be restructured, we developed two mid-fi wireframes and prototypes using Figma. One prototype featured a quick form directly on the landing page that allows user to input the only necessary information to search for a tutor; while the other brings users through a questionnaire step-by-step for a more personalized experience.

Usability Testing

The two mid-fi prototypes were then tested through user interviews, allowing us to gather valuable feedback on their preferences, pain points, and overall experience with the design. This testing phase provided critical insights into areas for improvement and helped validate the design choices before moving to the final stages.



Synthesis

The feedback collected from the usability tests were then synthesized into key findings and insights, such as:

Customized Search Results Are More Enjoyable

Users find personalized results based on their input to be satisfying. Customization made the platform feel more user-centric and tailored to their individual needs, increasing engagement and trust.

Positive Reactions to Flexible Options

Users appreciated the ability to skip questions or delay certain steps, such as providing payment details. This flexibility allowed them to move at their own pace, reducing friction and making the sign-up process feel less daunting.

Quick Form vs Questionnaire Format

While users found the questionnaire-style format to be more personal and engaging, they also appreciated the simplicity and efficiency of the quick form. To address these conflicting preferences, I proposed a solution to combine the best of both approaches. This would allow users to choose the experience that best suited their needs, and introduced a flexibility that would cater to a broader range of users and enhance overall satisfaction with the sign-up process.

Ready to Bloom

Study Buds is a place where students receive the intellectual nourishment they need to blossom into their full potential. Inspired by the concept of springtime flower buds, the rebranding embraces a visual language centered on growth, renewal, and flourishing.
